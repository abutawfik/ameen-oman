01 · Foundation

Brand Identity

Al-Ameen (الأمين) means "the trustworthy" in Arabic — an honorific historically applied to those entrusted with what cannot be lost. The platform is the nation's trusted guardian at the border, and every design decision earns that name.

Positioning

Sovereign, trustworthy, insightful

Al-Ameen is a national-grade intelligence platform operated by border authorities. The brand carries the weight of state decisioning without feeling militarized — it communicates competence, care, and clarity. For executives (Inspector General, ministry), the brand reads as credible and ceremonial. For operators (border officers, analysts), it reads as calm, legible, and fast.

Brand Promise

"Every decision is explainable."

Where other systems say flagged, Al-Ameen says flagged because — these signals, from these sources, with this confidence. The brand expression follows: hierarchy over noise, attribution over assertion, transparency as a feature.

Pillar · 01

Sovereign

Oman-rooted, state-grade. Deep midnight base, Oman Royal Red accents, Frankincense Gold for ceremonial moments. Never generic, never "startup."

Pillar · 02

Trustworthy

Type hierarchy is clear. Arabic and English carry equal weight. Risk verdicts cite their sources. White space signals seriousness, not emptiness.

Pillar · 03

Insightful

The interface surfaces why before what. Explainability is the product. Visuals privilege contribution breakdowns, signal attribution, and confidence bands.

02 · Foundation

Logo System

The mark is "The Guardian's Gate" — a shield-arch silhouette enclosing the Arabic letter Alif (ا, the first letter of الأمين), crowned by a vigilant crescent, standing on a frontier base line. Every element is meaningful and every lockup is bilingual-equal.

Clear space & minimum sizes

  • Clear space around the mark = 25% of mark height on all sides.
  • Minimum digital size: 24 px tall for the mark, 40 px tall for horizontal lockup.
  • Minimum print size: 10 mm tall for the mark.
  • Never place the mark on a background that reduces contrast below 3:1 between mark and canvas.

Don'ts

  • Don't recolor the mark outside the sanctioned palette.
  • Don't stretch, rotate, or skew.
  • Don't add effects — no drop shadows, outer glows, embosses.
  • Don't lock up with other institutional marks without approval.
  • Don't place the Latin wordmark on its own without the Arabic in official contexts.

14 · Reference

Voice & Language

Al-Ameen speaks with the calm, cited authority of a senior analyst. Never alarmist. Never casual. Bilingual where it matters — Arabic and English equal peers, not parent/translation.

Do

  • Cite the source with every flag — "flagged because GDELT reports elevated conflict score…"
  • Use routine / low / elevated / high / critical — not "amber/red" or "threat level 5."
  • Address officers by rank: "Major Al-Balushi," "Colonel…"
  • Pair Arabic + English for titles, hero copy, critical alerts, footer; English only for dense UI chrome is OK.
  • State uncertainty plainly: "Medium confidence — signal may update."

Don't

  • "🚨 WARNING" — no emoji in operator UI. Icons only.
  • "Suspicious traveler detected" — too assertive. Use "elevated risk signals" or "referred for secondary."
  • "Powered by AI" — the word is risk engine. The ML is an overlay, not the brand.
  • Slang, contractions in executive/IG surfaces. "Do not" not "don't."
  • Arabic-as-afterthought. Never smaller than 80% of the English equivalent at the same hierarchy level.
15 · Reference

Accessibility

Al-Ameen targets WCAG 2.1 AA. Every chosen color pair in this guide meets the required contrast for its role. Every control has visible focus, every interactive target is at least 32×32 CSS px.

Contrast

Body text carries ≥4.5:1. Large text ≥3:1. Risk tier chips ≥4.5:1 against their surface.

Focus

Gold outline + 2 px offset. Never rely on color alone — shape or weight changes always accompany color.

RTL

Full RTL support via [dir="rtl"]. Icons, layouts, and navigation mirror in Arabic locale.
